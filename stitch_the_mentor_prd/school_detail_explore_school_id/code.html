About Cambridge

The University of Cambridge is a collegiate research university in Cambridge, United Kingdom. Founded in 1209 and granted a royal charter by Henry III in 1231, Cambridge is the second-oldest university in the English-speaking world and the world's fourth-oldest surviving university.

Cambridge has a long history of academic excellence and is consistently ranked among the top universities in the world. It is a collegiate university, meaning that it is made up of self-governing and independent colleges, each with its own property and income. The university is home to over 100 libraries, which hold a total of over 15 million books.
